Dive Into Traditional Arts:
Drawing Basics


Before diving into traditional arts like painting and drawing,
it's important to have a foundational understanding of several key concepts and skills.

Here are the basics to know:






Materials and Tools











Drawing: Pencils (various hardnesses),
erasers, paper, charcoal, graphite sticks, ink pens, and sketchbooks.












Painting: Brushes, canvases, palette knives,
palettes, and paints (watercolors, oils, acrylics).







Basic Techniques











Drawing Techniques: Shading, hatching, cross-hatching
, stippling, and blending.












Painting Techniques: Color mixing, layering, glazing,
scumbling, and impasto.







Understanding Light and Shadow











Light Source: Identify where the light is coming from.












Shading:Learn how to create gradients and depict
shadows and highlights to give depth and dimension.


Color Theory











Primary Colors: Red, blue, yellow.



Secondary Colors:Green, orange, purple
(created by mixing primary colors).



Tertiary Colors: Mixing primary and secondary colors.



Complementary Colors: Colors opposite each other on the color wheel
(e.g., blue and orange) which create contrast.



Analogous Colors: Colors next to each other on the color
wheel, creating harmony.







Composition and Layout











Rule of Thirds: Dividing the canvas into a 3x3 grid
to create balanced and interesting compositions.












Focal Point: Deciding where the viewer's eye should be drawn.












Balance and Symmetry: Creating visual stability in your artwork.


Perspective











Linear Perspective: Techniques for creating the illusion of depth using converging lines.












Atmospheric Perspective: Creating depth by changing color, value, and clarity to mimic how objects appear from a distance.







Proportion and Anatomy











Human Figure: Understanding the proportions of the human body.












Still Life and Objects: Observing and accurately depicting the sizes and relationships between objects.







Art History and Styles











Movements: Familiarize yourself with major art movements (e.g., Impressionism, Realism, Abstract).

Artists: Study the works and techniques of influential artists.


Practice and Observation






Sketching: Regularly practice sketching from life and photographs.


Observation: Develop a keen eye for detail by closely observing your subjects.







Creativity and Personal Expression






Inspiration: Find what inspires you and incorporate that into your work.


Style: Develop your unique artistic style through experimentation and practice.


By mastering these fundamental concepts and skills, you'll build a solid foundation for your journey into traditional arts like painting and drawing.
