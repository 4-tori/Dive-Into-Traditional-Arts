Paper



Different types of paper can greatly influence the outcome of your drawings.

Here are guides to various types of drawing paper, when to use them, and their purposes:






Sketch Paper





Characteristics:

Lightweight: Typically around 50-65 lb (74-96 gsm).
Smooth Surface: Allows for easy sketching and erasing.

Usage and Purpose:

Preliminary Sketches:Ideal for quick sketches, practice, and rough drawings.
Planning:Useful for working out compositions and ideas before committing to more expensive or specialized paper.






Drawing Paper





Characteristics:

Medium Weight: Around 70-100 lb (104-150 gsm).
Varied Texture: Available in smooth, medium, or rough textures.

Usage and Purpose:

Finished Drawings:Suitable for final works due to its durability and texture.
Versatile Use:Works well with various dry media like graphite, charcoal, colored pencils, and ink.






Bristol Paper





Characteristics:

Heavyweight: Typically around 100-300 lb (200-600 gsm).
Smooth or Vellum Surface: Smooth for detailed work; vellum for textured effects.

Usage and Purpose:

Detailed Drawings:Smooth surface is perfect for fine, detailed work with pen, ink, and markers.
Mixed Media:Vellum surface can handle various media, including light watercolor washes.






Watercolor Paper





Characteristics:

Heavyweight: Usually 140-300 lb (300-640 gsm).
Textured Surface: Available in hot press (smooth), cold press (medium texture), and rough (high texture).

Usage and Purpose:

Watercolor Paintings:Designed to handle the wetness of watercolor without buckling.
Mixed Media:Cold press and rough textures are excellent for combining watercolors with ink, pencil, and pastels.




Tips for Choosing the Right Paper:



Consider the Medium:

Different papers are designed to work best with specific media (e.g., watercolor paper for watercolors, charcoal paper for charcoal).

Think About Texture:

The texture (or tooth) of the paper affects how media adhere and the overall look of the artwork.

Weight Matters:

Heavier papers are more durable and less likely to buckle or warp with wet media.

Purpose and Final Use:

Choose high-quality, archival papers for final artworks, and more economical options for practice and sketches.
